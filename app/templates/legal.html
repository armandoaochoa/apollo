Electronic Signature Legality

Global adoption of electronic signatures has enabled organizations and individuals alike to enter into legal agreements without the hassle of printing, signing, scanning, and in our case, fretting about the secure storage and transfer of sensitive documents and then wondering if your confidential information can be trusted in the hands of another.

Over 60 countries and counting have regulated and accepted electronic signatures. We’ve included a sample of the major ones and legal reference if you’re ever curious. Many more countries have crafted law regarding e-signatures based on the UNCITRAL Model Law on Electronic Signatures from the United Nations.

Rest assured that UnderNDA exclusively facilitates legally binding, digitally executed contracts for your Non-Disclosure Agreement needs with a full audit log accompanying each contract to back it up.

United States: E-Sign Act

United States: Uniform Electronic Transactions Act

Canada: Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA)

United Kingdom: Electronic Communications Act

United Kingdom: Electronic Signatures Regulation

Australia: Electronic Transactions Act

European Union: EU Directive

India: Information Technology Act

Japan: Law Concerning Electronic Signatures and Certification Services

China: Electronic Signature Law of the People’s Republic of China

New Zealand: Electronic Transactions Act
